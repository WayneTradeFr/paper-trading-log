Learning risk management through paper trading. 16yo aspiring quant trader.

# My Trading Log

I am 16. I learn trading on a paper account since June 2025.
Goal: understand risk before building trading programs later.

## What I did

**June 2025 - Oct 2025: Starting**
I blew up 10+ demo accounts.
Why: no stop loss, revenge trading after losses, position size too big.
What I learned: without rules, you lose 100% of the time.

**31 Oct 2025 - Today: 10M account**
I opened a 10M demo account to practice.
My rule: risk 1% max per trade = 100k$.

## My big mistake - March 2026

**What happened:**
1. Account went to 19M.
2. War started, I lost 9M fast. Back to 10M.
3. I was angry. I put 90% on Bitcoin short, with no stop loss.
4. Bitcoin went down, I went back to 20M.

**The problem:**
If Bitcoin went up only 6%, my account went to 0. I was lucky.
This was not trading. This was a bet. I broke my 1% rule.

**Why I did it:**
I was angry after losing 9M. I wanted money back fast.
I knew the rule but I did not follow it.

## What I change now

1. **I close the 10M account.** It taught me bad habits.
2. **I start a 100k$ account.** More real. 1% risk = 1,000$ max per trade.
3. **New mandatory rule:** I set stop loss BEFORE I click buy/sell. No stop = no trade.
4. **I track mistakes:** Every time I break a rule, I write it here.

**March 2026: Rules broken = 1.** Goal for April 2026 = 0.

## Why I show my mistakes

Making +100% means nothing if you take crazy risk.
Showing I almost lost everything 2 times, and the rules I made to stop it, has value.
My goal is to learn how not to lose money. Not to be lucky.
